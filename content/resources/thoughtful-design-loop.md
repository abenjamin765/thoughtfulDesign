# The Thoughtful Design Loop

The operating framework that runs through the whole course. It is a loop, not a line — real work circles back as you learn.

## The seven steps

| Step | Question | What you do |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Model | What exists? | Map the objects, relationships, and forces in the system |
| Understand | Who is affected? | Learn what people need and what reality is asking |
| Align | What decision are we making? | Frame the decision and get the team to a shared choice |
| Shape | What should the experience become? | Craft clear, usable, accessible interactions |
| Test | What did we learn? | Put the design in front of reality and gather evidence |
| Account | What consequences are we creating? | Trace second-order effects and ethical risk |
| Adapt | What should change next? | Revise based on what you learned |

> The loop is not a process you need permission to run.
> It is a set of questions a thoughtful designer keeps asking.

## Using it on real work

For any project, write one line per step. Where you cannot answer, you have found your next move — more modeling, more understanding, a decision to force, or a consequence to investigate.
