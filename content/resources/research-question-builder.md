# Research Question Builder

Use this to move from "we should do some research" to a focused, decision-driven study.

## Step 1 — Start from the decision

- **Decision we need to make:** ____________________
- **Riskiest assumption behind it:** ____________________
- **What would change our minds:** ____________________

## Step 2 — Write the research question

A research question states what the *team* needs to learn. Keep it open and answerable.

- Weak: "Do users like the new flow?"
- Strong: "Why do patients abandon booking before confirming?"

**Our research question:** ____________________

## Step 3 — Match the method

| If you need to know… | Use… |
| --- | --- |
| Why / how / what reasoning | Interviews, usability testing, contextual inquiry |
| How many / how often | Analytics, funnels, surveys at scale |
| Whether A beats B on a metric | A/B test |
| The real context of use | Contextual inquiry |

**Chosen method and why it fits:** ____________________

## Step 4 — Draft non-leading interview questions

Turn the research question into things you'd actually say to a participant — without planting answers.

- Ask about the past: "Walk me through the last time you…"
- Ask about the moment: "What were you trying to do when…?"
- Avoid: "Don't you find X frustrating?" / "Would you use this?"

## Quality check

- [ ] The research question ties to a real decision.
- [ ] The method matches the kind of uncertainty.
- [ ] No interview question leads the participant.
- [ ] The study is as small as it can be while still being able to change our minds.
