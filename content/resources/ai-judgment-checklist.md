# AI Judgment Checklist

Use this when you use AI in your own work, and when you design an AI feature for users.

## Part A — Using AI as a tool

- [ ] I gave a structured prompt: goal, context, constraints, and form of the answer.
- [ ] I checked the output for grounding (is it supported, or invented?).
- [ ] I checked for completeness (what did it leave out?).
- [ ] I checked for bias (whose perspective is assumed or erased?).
- [ ] I judged confidence against accuracy (does its sure tone match reality?).
- [ ] The decision and its correctness remain mine.

## Part B — Designing an AI feature

Map the workflow:

| What must the human decide? | |
| How does the user recover from a wrong output? | |

Design for trust:

- [ ] Confidence is communicated honestly (no false precision).
- [ ] The output's basis is visible (explainability).
- [ ] Errors are easy to notice, undo, or correct.
- [ ] A human checkpoint sits before any costly or irreversible action.
- [ ] The feature is accessible (AI text and controls work for varied users).

## The restraint question

- [ ] I considered whether *not* using AI here would serve users better. If AI stays, I can say why.
