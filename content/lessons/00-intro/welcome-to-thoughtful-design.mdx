## Why this course exists

Most design education teaches tools and surface process: how to use a tool, how to run a sprint, how to ship a screen. Those skills matter. But the work that increasingly defines a designer's value happens underneath the screen — in the systems, decisions, and consequences a product creates.

Thoughtful Design is about that deeper work. It prepares you to look at a complex product problem and ask better questions, find the underlying system, understand the people affected, make intentional decisions, and explain them clearly.

> This is not a course about making prettier screens.
> It is a course about shaping the systems those screens belong to.

## What you will learn

The course runs **eleven modules across four parts**:

1. **Seeing the System** — reframing UX from screens to systems, modeling objects, and thinking in systems.
2. **Understanding People and Decisions** — research, facilitation, and product strategy.
3. **Shaping Responsible Experiences** — interaction craft, accessibility, and ethics.
4. **Designing With Intelligence** — using AI with judgment and building a lasting practice.

## How each lesson works

Lessons are built to be read, not skimmed. Each one tends to follow the same rhythm: a realistic situation, the hidden problem beneath it, a reframe, a clear concept, and a small exercise. Along the way you will answer short inline checks that give you immediate feedback.

<Callout type="tip">

Inline checks are low-stakes. They are here to make your thinking active, not to grade you. Answer honestly — getting one "wrong" and reading the feedback is often where the learning happens.

</Callout>

## The framework that ties it together

One model recurs throughout the course: the **Thoughtful Design Loop**.

1. **Model** — What exists?
2. **Understand** — Who is affected?
3. **Align** — What decision are we making?
4. **Shape** — What should the experience become?
5. **Test** — What did we learn?
6. **Account** — What consequences are we creating?
7. **Adapt** — What should change next?

Every module connects back to one or more steps. By the end, the loop becomes your own operating model for real work.

<Reveal label="How to get the most from this course">

Bring a real product or problem from your own work and apply each module to it as you go. The exercises are designed to be done independently, and they are far more useful when the example is yours.

</Reveal>
