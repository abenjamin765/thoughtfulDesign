# Module 6 Brief — Product Strategy and Design Judgment

> Locked brief. Source: requirements §6, Module 6. Do not invent scope beyond this.

- **Part:** 2 — Understanding People and Decisions
- **Module ID:** `06-product-strategy`
- **Core claim:** Design judgment means choosing what matters most under real constraints.
- **Loop step(s):** Align, Shape
- **Recurring scenario:** Internal enterprise dashboard
- **Resource:** Design Rationale Template
- **Public preview lesson:** `three-good-directions`

## Purpose

Help learners connect UX decisions to product goals, constraints, tradeoffs, and business outcomes.

## Learning objectives

Learners should be able to:

- connect UX work to product strategy,
- identify product goals and constraints,
- evaluate tradeoffs,
- distinguish user needs from business goals,
- explain design rationale,
- make decisions with incomplete information.

## Key topics

- Product strategy
- Business goals
- UX metrics
- Product metrics
- Problem framing
- Opportunity framing
- Prioritization
- Tradeoffs
- Design principles
- Decision quality
- Satisficing under uncertainty
- Communicating rationale

## Opening story structure

Open with a designer who has three reasonable design directions but cannot choose among them because each serves a different goal. Focus on judgment, not taste.

## Inline check seeds

1. Which design direction best supports the stated business goal?
2. Which tradeoff is most important in this scenario?
3. Which rationale is strongest?
4. What information would most improve this decision?

## Guided practice

Learners write a short decision rationale: recommendation, user need, business goal, evidence, tradeoffs, risks, next step.

## Module assessment focus

Learners evaluate competing design options and select the strongest rationale.
