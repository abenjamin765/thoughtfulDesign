# Module 11 Brief — The Thoughtful Designer's Practice

> Locked brief. Source: requirements §6, Module 11. Do not invent scope beyond this.

- **Part:** 4 — Designing With Intelligence
- **Module ID:** `11-thoughtful-designers-practice`
- **Core claim:** The next generation of UX requires stronger thinking, not just stronger tools.
- **Loop step(s):** All seven (Model, Understand, Align, Shape, Test, Account, Adapt)
- **Recurring scenario:** AI writing / recommendation assistant (carried from Module 10)
- **Resource:** Design Decision Record
- **Public preview lesson:** `knowing-the-methods-isnt-enough`

## Purpose

Bring the course together by focusing on the designer's professional habits, communication, and reflective judgment.

## Learning objectives

Learners should be able to:

- describe the habits of a thoughtful designer,
- communicate design rationale clearly,
- reflect on uncertainty and tradeoffs,
- recognize when to push, pause, or compromise,
- document design decisions,
- apply the Thoughtful Design Loop to future work.

## Key topics

- Reflective practice
- Design maturity
- Communication
- Design documentation
- Critique
- Working with PMs and engineers
- Managing ambiguity
- Professional judgment
- Design principles
- Decision records
- Career growth
- The Thoughtful Design Loop

## Opening story structure

Open with a designer who knows the methods but struggles to navigate ambiguity, disagreement, imperfect evidence, and product pressure. Show that thoughtful design is not a single method — it is a way of practicing.

## Inline check seeds

1. Which update communicates the design rationale most clearly?
2. When should the designer push for more evidence?
3. When is compromise appropriate?
4. Which decision record is most useful?

## Guided practice

Learners complete a personal Thoughtful Design practice plan: what they will notice, what they will ask, what they will document, how they will use AI, how they will account for consequences.

## Module assessment focus

Learners analyze a complex design situation and choose the most thoughtful next step.

## Lesson plan (3 lessons)

1. **knowing-the-methods-isnt-enough** (preview) — methods-without-judgment story; the Shift.
2. **communicating-and-documenting-judgment** — rationale, decision records, working with PMs/engineers.
3. **your-thoughtful-design-practice** — full loop synthesis; Try It with Design Decision Record; bridge to Final Assessment.
