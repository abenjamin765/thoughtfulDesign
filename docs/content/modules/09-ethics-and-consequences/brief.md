# Module 9 Brief — Ethics and Product Consequences

> Locked brief. Source: requirements §6, Module 9. Do not invent scope beyond this.

- **Part:** 3 — Shaping Responsible Experiences
- **Module ID:** `09-ethics-and-consequences`
- **Core claim:** Thoughtful designers consider what their work makes easier, harder, normal, hidden, rewarded, or risky.
- **Loop step(s):** Account
- **Recurring scenario:** Marketplace onboarding flow
- **Resource:** Ethical Risk Review
- **Public preview lesson:** `the-conversion-win-with-a-cost`

## Purpose

Give learners a practical framework for identifying ethical risks in everyday product decisions.

## Learning objectives

Learners should be able to:

- identify ethical risk in product experiences,
- recognize manipulative patterns,
- evaluate consent, transparency, and privacy concerns,
- analyze who benefits and who bears cost,
- distinguish persuasion from manipulation,
- recommend more transparent alternatives.

## Key topics

- Design ethics
- Dark patterns
- Privacy
- Consent
- Transparency
- Persuasive design
- Manipulative design
- Power asymmetry
- Trust
- Data visibility
- Behavioral nudges
- User dignity
- Business pressure

## Opening story structure

Open with a product decision that improves conversion while quietly reducing user control, clarity, or trust. Avoid cartoonish villainy — the ethical problem should feel realistic and defensible from a business perspective.

## Inline check seeds

1. Which pattern is most manipulative?
2. What information is being hidden from the user?
3. Who benefits from this design?
4. Which alternative preserves both business value and user trust?

## Guided practice

Learners conduct an ethical risk review: intended outcome, affected users, possible harms, power imbalance, missing transparency, safer alternative.

## Module assessment focus

Learners evaluate a conversion-focused product flow and identify ethical risks, tradeoffs, and better design options.
