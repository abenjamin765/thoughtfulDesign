# Module 4 Brief — Research as Sensemaking

> Locked brief. Source: requirements §6, Module 4. Do not invent scope beyond this.

- **Part:** 2 — Understanding People and Decisions
- **Module ID:** `04-research-as-sensemaking`
- **Core claim:** Research is not just validation. It is how teams learn what reality is asking of them.
- **Loop step(s):** Understand, Test
- **Recurring scenario:** Healthcare appointment portal
- **Resource:** Assumption Audit (primary), Research Question Builder (secondary)
- **Public preview lesson:** `we-need-to-validate-this`

## Purpose

Position UX research as a practical way to reduce uncertainty and make better product decisions.

## Learning objectives

Learners should be able to:

- distinguish research questions from interview questions,
- identify assumptions and risks,
- choose appropriate research methods,
- recognize weak evidence,
- synthesize observations into insights,
- connect research findings to design decisions.

## Key topics

- Research as decision support
- Assumptions
- Research questions
- Interviewing
- Usability testing
- Contextual inquiry
- Surveys
- Qualitative vs. quantitative evidence
- Synthesis
- Evidence strength
- Research ethics

## Opening story structure

Open with a team saying "We need to validate this design," then reveal they have not agreed on the riskiest assumption, the decision they need to make, or what evidence would change their mind.

## Inline check seeds

1. Which of these is a research question?
2. Which of these is a leading interview question?
3. What method best fits this uncertainty?
4. Which finding is supported by evidence?

## Guided practice

Learners complete an assumption audit: What do we believe? Why? What evidence do we have? What would we need to learn? What decision will the research inform?

## Module assessment focus

Learners choose research methods and critique sample findings from a product scenario.
