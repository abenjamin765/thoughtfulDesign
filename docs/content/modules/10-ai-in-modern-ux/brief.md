# Module 10 Brief — AI in Modern UX

> Locked brief. Source: requirements §6, Module 10. Do not invent scope beyond this.

- **Part:** 4 — Designing With Intelligence
- **Module ID:** `10-ai-in-modern-ux`
- **Core claim:** AI can accelerate design work, but thoughtful designers remain responsible for judgment.
- **Loop step(s):** Understand, Shape, Account
- **Recurring scenario:** AI writing / recommendation assistant
- **Resource:** AI Judgment Checklist
- **Public preview lesson:** `the-polished-answer-that-was-wrong`

## Purpose

Teach learners how to use AI in UX work and how to design AI-powered experiences responsibly.

## Learning objectives

Learners should be able to:

- identify useful and risky applications of AI in UX work,
- use AI to support research, synthesis, modeling, ideation, critique, and writing,
- evaluate AI output critically,
- design human-in-the-loop experiences,
- account for uncertainty, error, and trust,
- decide when not to use AI.

## Key topics

- AI as tool
- AI as design material
- AI as system actor
- Prompting as structured thinking
- AI-assisted research
- AI-assisted OOUX
- AI-assisted critique

- Responsible AI
- AI and accessibility

## Opening story structure

Open with a designer using AI to quickly generate a polished solution that turns out to be incomplete, generic, biased, or disconnected from evidence. Then show how a thoughtful designer uses AI as a collaborator, not an authority.

## Inline check seeds

1. Which UX task is AI most appropriate for in this scenario?
2. Which AI output should be treated with the most caution?
3. What should the user know before trusting this AI recommendation?
4. Where should human review remain in the system?

## Guided practice

Learners critique an AI-assisted workflow: where AI helps, where it may mislead, what evidence is needed, what the human must decide, how the user should recover from error.

## Module assessment focus

Learners evaluate an AI-powered product concept for usefulness, trust, transparency, error handling, and ethical risk.
