# Module 5 Brief — Facilitation for Designers

> Locked brief. Source: requirements §6, Module 5. Do not invent scope beyond this.

- **Part:** 2 — Understanding People and Decisions
- **Module ID:** `05-facilitation-for-designers`
- **Core claim:** A thoughtful designer does not only create artifacts. They create conditions for better decisions.
- **Loop step(s):** Align
- **Recurring scenario:** Internal enterprise dashboard
- **Resource:** Facilitation Planner
- **Public preview lesson:** `the-design-review-that-went-nowhere`

## Purpose

Teach learners how to help teams align, make decisions, and move through ambiguity without relying on formal authority.

## Learning objectives

Learners should be able to:

- define the purpose of facilitation in design work,
- identify when a meeting needs facilitation,
- structure a design conversation around a decision,
- create better prompts,
- surface disagreement productively,
- distinguish discussion from decision-making.

## Key topics

- Facilitation as a design skill
- Stakeholder alignment
- Workshop design
- Meeting design
- Decision framing
- Critique
- Prioritization
- Tradeoff conversations
- Decision records
- Handling ambiguity

## Opening story structure

Open with a design review where everyone gives feedback, no one agrees, and the designer leaves with more confusion than clarity. Then show how facilitation changes the conversation.

## Inline check seeds

1. What decision is this meeting actually trying to make?
2. Which prompt is most likely to produce useful feedback?
3. Which stakeholder is missing from the conversation?
4. Which response best surfaces the tradeoff?

## Guided practice

Learners design a self-contained meeting plan: purpose, decision needed, participants, prompts, activities, expected output.

## Module assessment focus

Learners diagnose a broken meeting and choose facilitation moves to improve it.

## Lesson plan (3 lessons)

1. **the-design-review-that-went-nowhere** (preview) — unfocused critique story; the Shift.
2. **framing-the-decision** — decision framing; discussion vs. decision; who is in the room.
3. **prompts-disagreement-and-decisions** — better prompts; surfacing disagreement; Try It with Facilitation Planner; bridge to Module 6.
